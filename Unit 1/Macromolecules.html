Introduction to Biological Macromolecules

Molecular Diversity Due to Carbon

Variations in carbon skeletons allows for molecular diversity.

Carbon can form large molecules known as macromolecules.

Four classes for macromolecules (molecules made of smaller subunits):

  • Carbohydrates
  • Proteins
  • Nucleic Acids
  • Lipids
Formation and Breakdown of Macromolecules

Polymers: chain-like macromolecules of similar or identical repeating units that are covalently bonded together

Monomers: the repeating units that make up polymers

Dehydration Synthesis: bonds two monomers with the loss of H2O to form a polymer

  • A + B → AB + H2O

Hydrolysis: breaks the bonds of polymer by adding H2O

  • AB + H2O → A + B
