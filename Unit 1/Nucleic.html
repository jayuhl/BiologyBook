Nucleic Acids

  • Function to: store, transmit, and express hereditary information
  • Two forms: Deoxyribonucleic Acid (DNA), Ribonucleic Acid (RNA)
Components of Nucleic Acids

Nucleotides → Polynucleotides → Nucleic Acids

Nitrogenous Base

Two types: Pyramidines & Purines

Pyrimidines:

  • one ring
  • 6 atoms
  • Examples:
    • DNA → Cytosine & Thymine
    • RNA → Cytosine & Uracil

Purines:

  • one ring with 6 atoms AND one ring with 5 atoms
  • Examples:
    • Adenine
    • Guanine


Five Carbon Sugar
  • Sugar bonded to a base
  • DNA → deoxyribose sugar
  • RNA → ribose sugar


Phosphate Group
  • Phosphate group added to 5' carbon of sugar to form nucleotide



Polynucleotides

Phosphate groups link adjacent nucleotides.

The sequence of bases along the DNA or mRNA is unique for each gene.

DNA
  • Consists of 2 polynucleotides
  • Double Helix
    • Antiparallel
    • hydrogen bonds between bases
  • Nitrogenous Pairs:
    • Cytosine + Guanine
    • Thymine + Adenine

RNA
  • Single-stranded polynucleotide
  • variable in shape due to base pairing within RNA
  • Nitrogenous Pairs:
    • Adenine + Uracil
    • Cytosine + Guanine
