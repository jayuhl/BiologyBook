Proteins

Protein: molecules consisting of polypeptides (polymers of amino acids) folded into a 3D shape

  • Comprised of the elements: C, H, O, N, S
  • Shape determines function.
Amino Acids:
  • Molecules that have amino group and carboxyl group.
    • 20 different amino acids
  • General Structure:
    • R = Variable side chain + Amino Group + Carboxyl Group
  • Examples:
    • Alanine
    • Glycine
  • Each amino acid has unique side chains that determine its chemical and physical properties. Side chains can be polar (hydrophilic), nonpolar (hydrophobic), or ionic (charged/hydrophilic).
  • Side chains interact, which determines the shape and function of the protein.
Formation of Peptide Bonds

To form a peptide bond, the carboxyl group of one AA must be positioned next to the amino group of another AA.


Polypeptides

Polypeptides: many amino acids linked by peptide bonds.

  • Polypeptides had a unique sequence of AAs and directionality.
  • Each end is chemically unique.
    • Free amino group (N-Terminus)
    • Free carboxyl group (C-Terminus)

The sequence of amino acids determines the 3-D shape.

When a polepeptide twists and folds (because of R-group interaction) it forms a protein.

Functions of Proteins:
  • Antibody: help protect body from disease
  • Enzyme: carry out chemical reactions or assist in creating new molecules or break down molecules
  • Messenger: transmit signals (i.e. hormones, insulin)
  • Structural: provide structure and support
  • Transport/Storage: bind to and carry small atoms and moelcules through the body
Levels of Protein Structure
Primary
  • sequence of amino acids
  • determined via genes
  • linear chain of amino acids
  • dictates secondary, tertiary, quaternary structure
Secondary
  • coils and folds due to hydrogen bonding within the polypeptide backbone
  • α-Helix: hydrogen bonding with every 4th amino acid
  • β-pleated sheet: hydrogen bonding between polypeptide chains lying side by side
Tertiary
  • 3-D folding due to interactions between side chains of amino acids
Quaternary
  • Association of 2 or more polypeptides
  • found only in some proteins
