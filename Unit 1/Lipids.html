Lipids

Lipids: do not include TRUE polymers

  • Small in size → not macromolecules
  • lipids are nonpolar → hydrophobic

Types of Lipids:

  • Fats
  • Phospholipids
  • Steroids
Fats

Fats are composed of glycerol and fatty acids.

Glyercol: classified as an alcohol (hydroxyl groups)

Fatty Acids: long, carbon chains (carboxyl group at one end)

3 fatty acids join to a glycerol vida ester linkage.

  • Ester linkage - bond between hydroxyl and carboxyl group

Saturated Fatty Acid: NO double bonds between carbons in carbon chain → saturated with hydrogen

Unsaturated Fatty Acid: one or more double bonds in carbon backbone

Phospholipids

Major component of cell membranes

  • 2 fatty acids attached to glycerol and phosphate group

Assemble as a bilayer in H2O

  • Tails → hydrophobic
  • Heads → hydrophilic


Steroids

Lipids with 4 fused rings

  • Unique groups attached to each ring determine type of steroid
