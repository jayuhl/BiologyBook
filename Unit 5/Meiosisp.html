Meiosis Overview

Meiosis: a process that creates haploid gamete cells in sexually reproducing diploid organisms

  • Results in daughter cells with half the number of chromosomes as the parent cell
    • Example: humans
      • Diploid: 2n= 46
      • Meiosis produces sperm and eggs that are haploid: n=23
  • Involves two rounds of division
    • Meiosis I and Meiosis II

There are three key events in meiosis that are unique:

  1. Prophase I: synapsis and crossing over
  2. Metaphase I: tetrads (homologous pairs) line up at the metaphase plate
  3. Anaphase I: homologous pairs separate

Meiosis followed by fertilization ensures genetic diversity in sexually reproducing organisms and provides genetic variation that plays a role in natural selection.

This cellular process is driven by the interaction of subcellular components and uses free energy that is required for the growth and reproduction of living systems
