Endosymbiont Theory

The endosymbiont theory explans the similarity between mitochondria and chloroplasts have with a prokaryote. It states that an early eukaryotic cell engulfed a prokaryotic cell.

  • Prokaryotic cell became an endosymbiont (cell that lives in another cell).
    • Together, they became one functional organism.

Evidence:

  • double membrane
  • ribosomes
  • circular DNA (plasmids)
  • capable of functioning on their own
