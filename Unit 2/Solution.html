Types of Solutions

Isotonic
  • No NET movement of water
  • Water diffuses into the cell at the same rate as water moves out of the cell.
Hypertonic
  • Cell will lose water to its surroundings
  • Water will move into extracellular fluid
  • Cell shrivels and can die
Hypotonic
  • Cell will gain water
  • concentration of nonpenetrating solutes is lower outside of the cell
  • Animal cells will swell and lyse
  • Plant cells will work optimally
