Passive Transport

Passive transport: transport of a molecule that does not require energy from the cell because a solute is moving with its concentration gradient.

  • involved in the important of materials and export of waste
  • Ex. Diffusion, Osmosis, Facilitated Diffusion
Diffusion
  • Spontaneous process resulting from the constant motion of molecules
  • Substances move from high to low concentration
    • Move DOWN the concentration gradient
  • Molecules diffuse directly across the membrane
    • There are different rates of diffusion for different molecules.
Osmosis
  • Diffusion of water down the concentration gradient across a selectively permeable membrane
    • Areas of high water/low solute concentration to areas of low water/high solute conentration
Facilitated Diffusion
  • Diffusion of molecules through the membrane via transport proteins
  • Increases the rate of diffusion for: small ions, water, carbohydrates, etc.
  • Two categories od transport proteins: channel and carrier
    • Channel Proteins: provide channel for proteins and ions to pass through

    • Carrier Proteins: undergo conformational changes for substances to pass
