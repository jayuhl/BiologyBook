Endomembrane Organelles

Nucleus
  • contains chromosomes (genetic information)
  • enclosing by nuclear envelop → double membrane
  • has pores → pores regulate entry and exit of materials from the nucleus
  • contains a nucleolus
    • dense region of nucleus where ribosomal RNA (rRNA) is synthesized
      • rRNA is combined with proteins to form large and small subunits of ribosomes
      • subunits exit via nuclear pores
      • subunits assemble into Ribosomes
        • Ribosomes translate messages found on mRNA into the primary structure of polypeptides.
Ribosomes

Comprised of ribosomal RNA and protein

  • Function: synthesize proteins

Can be found in two locations:

  1. Cytosol
    • proteins produced here generally function only within cytosol (enzymes)
    • "Free Ribosomes"
  2. Bound to the endoplasmic reticulum or nuclear envelop
    • can be secreted from cell via transport vesicles
    • antibodies, hormones (insulin), etc. are secreted
Endoplasmic Reticulum

A network of membranous sacs and tubes

  • Functions: synthesize membranes
    • compartmentalize cell to keep proteins formed in the rough ER separate from those of free ribosomes

Two types: Rough and Smooth

  1. Rough ER
    • countains ribosomes boud to ER membrane
  2. Smooth ER
    • no ribosomes
    • synthesizes lipids, metabolizes carbohydrates, detoxifies the cell
Golgi Complex

Contains flattened membranous sacs called cisternae

  • separate sacs from cytosol
  • Each cisternae is not connected
  • has directionality
    • Cis Face: recieves vesicles from ER
    • Trans Face: sends vesicles back to cytosol to other locations or to plasma membrane for secretion

Functions:

  • Recieves transport vesicles with materials from ER
  • Modifies proteins → makes sure proteins are folded correctly
  • adds molecular tags
  • sort materials
  • packages materials into new transport vesicles that exit the membrane via exocytosis
Lysosomes

Membranous sac with hydrolytic enzymes

Function: hydrolyzes macromolecules in animal cells

  • Autophagy: lysosomes can recycle their own cells organic material
    • allows cell to renew itself
Peroxisomes
  • Membrane-bound metabolic compartment
  • similar to lysosomes
  • catalyze reactions that produce H2O2
    • Enzymes in peroxisomes then breakdown H2O2 to H2O
Vacuoles

Large vesicles that stem from the ER and Golgi

  • selective in transport

Types:

  • Food Vacuole: form via Phagocytosis (cell eating) and then are digested by lysosomes
  • Contractile Vacuole: maintain water in cells
  • Central Vacuole: found only in plants
    • contains inorganic ions and water
