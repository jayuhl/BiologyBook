Fluid Mosaic Model

A model to describe the structure of cell membranes

    Fluid: Membrane is held together by weak hydrophobic interactions and can thereforce move and shift
    • Temperature affects fluidity
      • Unsaturated hydrocarbon tails help maintain fluidity at low temperatures.
      • Kinked tails prevent tight packing of phospholipids.
Cholesterol helps maintain fluidity at high and low temperatures
  • High Temperature: reduces movement
  • Low temperatures: reduces tight packing of phospholipids
Mosaic comprised of many macromolecules
