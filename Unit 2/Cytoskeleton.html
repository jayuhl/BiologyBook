The Cytoskeleton

A network of fibers throughout the cytoplasm

  • Give structural support (especially for animal cells) and mechanical support (movement)
    • Anchor organelles
    • Allow movement of vesicles and organelles and/or the whole cell
      • Movement occurs when the cytoskeleton interacts with motor proteins

There are 3 types of fibers in the cyoskeleton:

  1. Microtubules
  2. Microfilaments
  3. Intermediate Filaments
Microtubules

Hollow, rod-like structures made from the protein tubulin

  • Grow from the centrosome
    • Assist in microtubule assembly
  • Functions:
    • structural support (tracks) for the movement of organelles that are interacting with motor proteins
    • assist in separation of chromosomes during cell division
    • cell motility (cilia & flagella)
      • sperm has flagella
      • trachea and protists have cilia (hair-like projections)
Microfilaments

Thin rods made of the protein actin

Functions:

  • Maintain cell shape â†’ bear tension
  • Assist in muscle contraction and cell motility
    • Actin works with another protein called myosin to cause a contraction
  • Division of animal cells
    • contractile ring of the cleavage furrow
Intermediate Filaments

Fibrous proteins made up of varying subunits that form the permanent structural elements of cells.

Functions:

  • Maintain cell shape
  • Anchor nucleus and organelles
  • Form nuclear lamina (coating inside nuclear envelop)
